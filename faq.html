Frequently Asked Questions

How does Clintonville Neighborhood Greenways make it safer for me and my family to bike and walk in the neighborhood?
Safety in numbers, awareness, slower traffic and less traffic.
What neighborhood destinations does Clintonville Neighborhood Greenways connect to?
8 Schools, the Whetstone library and rec center, Whetstone Park of Roses, soccer fields and ball fields, Clintonville farmers market, 6 churches, High Street shopping and restaurants. Multiple connection points to the Olentangy trail.
How does the CNG compliment the Olentangy Trail?
The Olentangy trail is great, but it's not practical for short trips within Clintonville. We need a network of safe roads that connect the neighborhood. This will make it easy for families to get around biking and walking, reduce traffic and parking problems, and increase patronage of local businesses.
Why do we want more people biking and walking in the neighborhood?
With more people biking and walking there are more opportunities for random encounters where neighbors can meet and interact. This makes the neighborhood safer and more pleasant for everyone.
How will this be funded?
The first phase of the neighborhood greenways project will be funded with Urban Infrastructure Renewal Funds (UIRF). These funds are allocated by the city for neighborhood improvements that have been identified and prioritized by residents of the neighborhood.
Why UIRF funds paying for something that doesn't benefit me?
Neighborhood greenways create a calmer and safer street environment that will benefit all Clintonville residents. This project was identified by the community in the 2009 Clintonville Neighborhood Plan and approved by the CAC and City for funding.
Why should people biking and walking have priority on these streets?
Because people biking and walking are the most vulnerable road users, they need to be given priority on neighborhood roads to ensure everyone's safety.
My street is already safe. Why do we need to do anything?
While some people feel safe on the streets as they are, many people, especially those with younger children, don't feel the roads are safe enough for biking and walking.
Will Neighborhood Greenways make the streets safer for my family?
The neighborhood greenways will primarily be used by families that live in the neighborhood. Whether walking, riding bikes, or driving, they can be trusted to use the streets safely.
How many people are going to be biking on my street?
We don't anticipate any more bike traffic on neighborhood greenways than current volume of car traffic.
Will I still be able to park on my street?
There are currently no plans to limit parking on neighborhood greenways.
Will the speed limit change on my street?
There are currently no plans to change speed limits.
Will I still be able to drive on my street?
The neighborhood greenway is shared network of streets. There are no plans to restrict any type of traffic.
Will it be confusing for people to navigate the street with new rules and signs?
There will be no new rules for people driving to follow. We anticipate way-finding signs to mark the route and sharrows to raise awareness of shared use roads.
I'm afraid that I'm going to hit a person biking or a person biking is going to hit me.
By raising awareness among all types of road users, neighborhood greenways are safer for everyone.
Will this invite strangers to come through my neighborhood?
Because this is a network of streets within Clintonville, the vast majority of people on the neighborhood greenways will live in the neighborhood.
Based on the Milton bike boulevard, people biking are reckless and dangerous. I don't want any more of these types of people in Clintonville.
Milton is a through route with many types of people, including some that are taking things a little too seriously. The neighborhood greenways will primarily be used by families that live in the neighborhood and are riding to local shops, restaurants, and parks.
Will this increase vandalism or crime by attracting more people to my street?
By creating more "eyes on the street" and a slower street environment, neighborhood greenways result in more awareness and community connections that are shown to reduce crime and vandalism.
